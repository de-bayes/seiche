WHY IT WORKS: THE DRIVER STUDY

Weather during the forecast window (cyan) carries 2 to 4x the signal of the buoy's own past (gray): air-water temperature gap, sustained wind (mixing), solar input, and the alongshore wind that drives upwelling.

ERROR ANATOMY OF THE BUOY-ONLY BASELINE

The earlier lags-only model: unbiased, sub-1°F medians, but its errors concentrated in the multi-degree swing days. The forecast-weather covariates were added specifically to attack that bin.

METHODOLOGY

Model

Five gradient-boosted quantile regressors (P5/P25/P50/P75/P95), horizon-conditioned: one model spans +1 h to +168 h with lead time as a feature, trained on ~280k stacked (time, horizon) rows. Forecasts are anchor-blended: the trajectory is shifted by the model's error against the current observation, decaying with an 8-hour timescale.

Data

NDBC 45174 hourly observations (2021 to present), ERA5 reanalysis as historical weather, GFS/HRRR blend as forecast weather, identical variables both sides. Features: water temp lags and trends, air-water gap, wind vectors and gusts, solar, seasonal and diurnal clocks, and forecast-window aggregates of each driver.

Validation

The last 35 days are an untouched test set; training stops 8 days earlier so no stacked row straddles the split. Persistence is reported at every lead. Quantile calibration is audited, not assumed. Caveat: training weather is reanalysis, so live skill at day 5 to 7 inherits the weather forecast's own error.

Open data: NOAA NDBC, ERA5 (Copernicus), GFS/HRRR via Open-Meteo. The buoy is seasonal, roughly May to November. Not an official product; for safety decisions use NWS Chicago.
